# Dig It: Video Keyframes Promotion Plan

## Positioning

Dig It turns local videos into useful still images: keyframes, cover frames, and step-by-step screenshots. The core message is "Video in. Keyframes out. No upload required."

## Audiences

- Content creators who need thumbnails, social post images, or video stills.
- Teachers, tutorial makers, and product teams who need step images from screen recordings or process videos.
- Sellers and makers who want clean cover images from product videos.
- Privacy-sensitive users who prefer local video processing.

## Channels

- App Store optimization: keep screenshots, preview images, subtitles, keywords, and privacy purpose strings aligned with the local-first value proposition.
- Website SEO: publish evergreen pages around "extract keyframes from video", "make a video cover image", and "turn video into step images".
- Short-form content: show before/after clips, tutorial workflows, and product demo examples.
- Apple Search Ads: start with exact-match intent keywords, then expand only when conversion data is acceptable.
- Cross-promotion: link from related Luo Peike apps and support pages where the use case is relevant.

## Content Priorities

- Explain that video processing happens locally and does not require uploading clips.
- Show concrete workflows: import video, choose mode, select frames, export PNGs, save history.
- Target practical search intent rather than generic "video editor" terms.
- Localize landing/help pages for existing App Store languages.

## Measurement

- Website: search impressions, organic clicks, App Store outbound clicks, and indexed page coverage.
- App Store: product page views, conversion rate, keyword rank, and regional conversion differences.
- Product: first import success rate, export/save usage, history reopen usage, and crash-free sessions.
